Stop-Loss and Exit Discipline for Crypto Strategies
Research Note Summary

This note summarizes robust stop and exit behavior for fast markets.

Core observations:
- Tight stops are frequently harvested by normal crypto noise.
- Wider, volatility-scaled stops reduce false exits but require smaller size.
- Time-based exits help recycle capital from stagnant positions.

Practical policy:
- Set initial stops from ATR multipliers by symbol class and regime.
- Require minimum stop distance to prevent structurally fragile setups.
- Add max-hold exits for strategies with bounded expected holding time.

Risk notes:
- Exit logic must be deterministic and auditable.
- Never relax stop enforcement after entry.
