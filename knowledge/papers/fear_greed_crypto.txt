Fear and Greed as a Context Signal in Crypto
Research Note Summary

This note summarizes how sentiment extremes can support contrarian setups.

Core observations:
- Extreme fear often coincides with oversold conditions and liquidity vacuums.
- Extreme greed is frequently associated with compressed future return distribution.
- Sentiment is a context variable, not a standalone entry trigger.

Practical use:
- Combine fear extremes with technical oversold evidence (RSI, z-score, VWAP deviation).
- Combine greed extremes with trend exhaustion and weak breadth confirmation.
- Downweight sentiment inputs during strongly trending directional regimes.

Execution guidance:
- Use sentiment to adjust confidence, not to bypass risk gates.
- Maintain strict stop discipline regardless of sentiment score.
