Seasonality and Calendar Effects in 24/7 Crypto Markets
Research Note Summary

This note summarizes weaker but still observable timing effects.

Core observations:
- Calendar effects exist but are less stable than in traditional markets.
- Weekend liquidity shifts can alter spread and slippage behavior.
- Session overlap and macro-event timing matter more than day-of-week alone.

Practical use:
- Use calendar context for execution quality adjustments, not primary alpha.
- Expect effect decay after public discovery.
- Keep core strategy logic regime-driven and price-driven.

Execution guidance:
- Increase slippage assumptions in thinner liquidity windows.
- Avoid overfitting entries to historical calendar quirks.
