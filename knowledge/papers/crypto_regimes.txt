Market Regime Classification in Digital Assets Using ADX and Volatility
Research Note Summary

This note summarizes practical regime classification rules for crypto markets.

Core observations:
- ADX between 20 and 25 is a useful transition zone between ranging and trending behavior.
- ATR percentage expansion often leads directional acceleration in the next few bars.
- Z-score extremes are more reliable in low-ADX environments than in high-ADX trends.

Practical rules:
- Trending up: ADX above threshold and +DI materially above -DI.
- Trending down: ADX above threshold and -DI materially above +DI.
- Volatile: ATR% elevated while ADX stays below trend threshold.
- Mean-reverting: low ADX with large absolute z-score.
- Ranging: default when no directional or volatility extreme dominates.

Implementation guidance:
- Recompute regime every bar.
- Smooth inputs with minimum lookback windows to reduce regime flicker.
- Attach confidence values so downstream sizing can adapt.
